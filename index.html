Project Overview

Project: Grandpa Tao Kitchen Menu Redesign

Goal: Transform dated laminated menu into a premium, modern Vietnamese bistro experience

Phase: 1 (Prototype) — 3–4 sample menus for owner feedback

Key Insight: Current laminated menu looks generic and undercuts food quality. Premium typography, curated photography, and thoughtful layout will elevate perceived value and customer spending.
02

Restaurant Context

Current Problems

  • Visual Overload: Too many photos, tiny text, inconsistent spacing
  • Cheap Perception: Laminated multi-page format undermines food quality
  • No Hierarchy: Everything competes for attention equally
  • Dated Aesthetic: Red binder with cluttered layout feels generic

Restaurant Details

  • Name: Grandpa Tao Kitchen
  • Location: 1190 N 2ND ST, EL CAJON, CA 92021
  • Cuisine: Vietnamese / Asian Fusion (Pho, Vermicelli, Rice Plates, Sushi, Drinks)
  • Type: Neighborhood casual dining
  • Menu Items: ~65 items across 8 categories
  • Price Range: $5–$18
03

Design Direction

Positioning

From: Generic laminated takeout menu

To: Modern Vietnamese Bistro (curated, editorial, warm, intentional)

Core Principles

  • Curated over Complete: One hero image per section, not scattered photos
  • Breathable Design: Aggressive whitespace signals premium quality
  • Editorial Aesthetic: Typography-first, photography as accent
  • Clear Hierarchy: Guide customers; control decision-making
  • Warm & Approachable: Inviting, not sterile
04

Visual Identity

Brand: Family-owned Vietnamese kitchen serving authentic, made-with-care dishes in a warm, welcoming neighborhood setting.

Visual Attributes

  • Warm, not cold
  • Editorial, not commercial
  • Contemporary, not trendy
  • Accessible, not pretentious
  • Intentional, not accidental

Photography Guidelines

Specs

  • Resolution: 300 DPI
  • Dimensions: 6.5" × 8.5" (full-bleed)
  • Color: Warm, slightly saturated (appetizing)
  • Format: High-res JPEG or RAW
12

Materials & Specs

Paper

  • Material: Matte synthetic (Cosmo, TerraSlate, PVC White Matte)
  • Weight: 14–15 mil thickness
  • Finish: Matte (not glossy)

Binding

  • Option A (Recommended): Hidden screw posts (premium, page-changeable)
  • Option B: Spiral coil (functional, lays flat)

Finishing

  • Gold foil stamping on cover (+$0.75–1.50 per unit)
  • Optional: Light debossing or embossing

Prototype Execution Plan

Phase 1: Design (2–3 days)

  1. Confirm all menu items, pricing with owner
  2. Gather/shoot 5 hero photos
  3. Design all 16 pages in InDesign or Canva
  4. Export high-quality PDF

Phase 2: Print (1–2 days)

  1. Get quotes from local printers
  2. Order 3–4 sample menus
  3. Inspect quality on delivery

Phase 3: Feedback (1–2 days)

  1. Present prototypes to owner
  2. Get specific feedback (hierarchy, descriptions, photos)
  3. Iterate if needed (reprint 1 revised version)

Phase 4: Production (5–7 days)
  • Finalize design changes
  • Place order for 50–100 menus
  • QA on delivery

    • Timeline: 10–14 days total
