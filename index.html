Restaurant Context

Current State Problems

  • Visual Overload: Too many photos, tiny text, inconsistent spacing
  • Cheap Perception: Laminated multi-page menu undermines food quality despite excellent cooking
  • Poor Hierarchy: No clear visual priority — everything competes for attention
  • Dated Aesthetic: Red binder frame and cluttered layout feel generic, not intentional

Menu Offerings

Categories: Starters, Vermicelli Bowls, Rice Plates, Noodles, Bánh Mì, Sushi, Drinks, Desserts

Estimated items: 60–80 total across all categories

Price range: $5–18 (entry-level accessible, mid-market casual dining)

Target Outcome

Transform perceived value from "takeout menu" to "modern Vietnamese bistro" — making customers feel they're choosing something curated and premium, not just ordering off a list.

03

Design Direction

Positioning

From: Laminated takeout menu (visual chaos)

To: Modern Vietnamese Bistro (editorial, curated, warm)

Core Principles

  • Curated over Complete: Show less, show it better. One hero image per section, not scattered thumbnails.
  • Breathable Design: Aggressive whitespace = premium perception
  • Editorial Aesthetic: Typography-driven, not photography-driven
  • Intentional Hierarchy: Guide the eye; control decision-making
  • Warm & Approachable: Not sterile or overly fancy — inviting and genuine

What we borrowed from these concepts

  • Recurring brand mark at the top of every section page — acts as wayfinding, signals "still in the same menu"
  • Shophouse line-art motif down the right edge of each interior page — quiet repeating decoration
  • Dark-green "House Favorite" callout boxes with gold corner accents — used for signature dishes
  • Hero-split layout (left photo / right list) — adopted for Vermicelli & Bánh Mì pages
  • Section icons (bowl, flame, baguette) next to subheads like "Grilled" and "Stir-fry"
  • Centered section headers with a small gold rule underneath

What we did NOT borrow

  • The dish data — every item, price, and code in these concepts is AI-invented. Our menu uses real Grandpa Táo data scraped from their Square Online site, verified against the printed menu in hand.
  • The "Grandma Tao" typo in the welcome spread (concept 14) — caught and corrected to "Grandpa Táo".
  • Vietnamese-name-led layout (concepts 08 & 09) — gorgeous but a comprehension trade-off. We went hybrid: English-led with Vietnamese italic underneath.
  • A hero spread for every category — too much. We picked four strategic heroes (intro, Chef's Phở, Shaking Beef, Vermicelli) plus one for Bánh Mì since customers need to see one to know what it is.

Price Analysis & Market Benchmarks

How Grandpa Táo's prices compare to similar restaurants in El Cajon and the broader San Diego market. The goal isn't to push prices up — it's to spot places where they're leaving money on the table without breaking the mom-and-pop feel customers come for.

Method: Vietnamese items compared against Vietnamese restaurants (El Cajon & SD-wide). Fusion items (sushi, teriyaki, Chinese-American stir-fries) compared against the appropriate non-Vietnamese benchmark. Suggested prices are modest adjustments — in most cases +$0 to +$2 — not a blanket repricing.

Visual Identity

Brand Positioning

Grandpa Tao's Kitchen is a neighborhood gathering place for authentic Vietnamese food made with family tradition and care. The menu should feel like an invitation to slow down and enjoy something genuine.

Visual Attributes

  • Warm, not cold
  • Editorial, not commercial
  • Contemporary, not trendy
  • Accessible, not pretentious
  • Intentional, not accidental

Design Influences: Modern Vietnamese cafés, upscale ramen bars, minimalist Japanese typography, editorial food layouts, luxury hotel menus, contemporary Asian bistros.

Spacing & Grid System

Page Margins

  • Outer margins: 0.75" (19mm) all sides
  • Inner margins (gutter): 0.5" (13mm) between two-page spreads
  • Top margin (first page): 1" to accommodate header breathing

Content Grid

  • Column system: 2-column (left menu / right imagery) or single-column
  • Baseline grid: 6pt (all text and elements align to 6pt grid)
  • Spacing unit: 12pt (all whitespace in multiples of 12pt)

Whitespace Rules

  • Between dish items: 18pt minimum (creates breathing room)
  • Between sections: 36pt minimum (visual separation)
  • Around images: 24pt minimum on all sides (images never touch edges)
  • Line height (body text): 1.6x (relaxed, premium feel)
Critical: Whitespace is not empty space — it's a design element. More whitespace = more premium perception. Never cram content.
