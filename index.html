Restaurant Context

Current State Problems

  • Visual Overload: Too many photos, tiny text, inconsistent spacing
  • Cheap Perception: Laminated multi-page menu undermines food quality despite excellent cooking
  • Poor Hierarchy: No clear visual priority — everything competes for attention
  • Dated Aesthetic: Red binder frame and cluttered layout feel generic, not intentional

Menu Offerings

Categories: Starters, Vermicelli Bowls, Rice Plates, Noodles, Bánh Mì, Sushi, Drinks, Desserts

Estimated items: 60–80 total across all categories

Price range: $5–18 (entry-level accessible, mid-market casual dining)

Target Outcome

Transform perceived value from "takeout menu" to "modern Vietnamese bistro" — making customers feel they're choosing something curated and premium, not just ordering off a list.

03

Design Direction

Positioning

From: Laminated takeout menu (visual chaos)

To: Modern Vietnamese Bistro (editorial, curated, warm)

Core Principles

  • Curated over Complete: Show less, show it better. One hero image per section, not scattered thumbnails.
  • Breathable Design: Aggressive whitespace = premium perception
  • Editorial Aesthetic: Typography-driven, not photography-driven
  • Intentional Hierarchy: Guide the eye; control decision-making
  • Warm & Approachable: Not sterile or overly fancy — inviting and genuine

Visual Identity

Brand Positioning

Grandpa Tao's Kitchen is a neighborhood gathering place for authentic Vietnamese food made with family tradition and care. The menu should feel like an invitation to slow down and enjoy something genuine.

Visual Attributes

  • Warm, not cold
  • Editorial, not commercial
  • Contemporary, not trendy
  • Accessible, not pretentious
  • Intentional, not accidental

Design Influences: Modern Vietnamese cafés, upscale ramen bars, minimalist Japanese typography, editorial food layouts, luxury hotel menus, contemporary Asian bistros.

Section Color Flow (Psychological Pacing)

  • Starters: Warm cream + subtle gold accents
  • Vermicelli: Fresh greens + botanical imagery
  • Rice Plates: Warm neutrals + terracotta highlights
  • Noodles: Deep browns + gold text (rich, indulgent)
  • Bánh Mì: Light cream + illustrated details (daytime, lighter)
  • Sushi: Minimal, centered on charcoal or cream
  • Drinks: Vibrant yet soft accents (celebration)
Color Strategy: Use color intentionally to guide emotion and pacing. Avoid monotone throughout — color variation between sections creates visual rhythm and prevents monotony.
07

Spacing & Grid System

Page Margins

  • Outer margins: 0.75" (19mm) all sides
  • Inner margins (gutter): 0.5" (13mm) between two-page spreads
  • Top margin (first page): 1" to accommodate header breathing

Content Grid

  • Column system: 2-column (left menu / right imagery) or single-column
  • Baseline grid: 6pt (all text and elements align to 6pt grid)
  • Spacing unit: 12pt (all whitespace in multiples of 12pt)

Whitespace Rules

  • Between dish items: 18pt minimum (creates breathing room)
  • Between sections: 36pt minimum (visual separation)
  • Around images: 24pt minimum on all sides (images never touch edges)
  • Line height (body text): 1.6x (relaxed, premium feel)
Critical: Whitespace is not empty space — it's a design element. More whitespace = more premium perception. Never cram content.
Total Pages: 16 (including cover and closing)
Format: 8.5" × 11" or 11" × 14" (standard letter size recommended)
Binding: Screw posts (hidden brass or stainless) or spiral coil
Paper: Matte synthetic (waterproof, tear-resistant, premium feel)

Materials & Production Specs

Paper Specification

PropertySpecification
MaterialMatte synthetic paper (Cosmo, TerraSlate, PVC White Matte)
Weight14–15 mil thickness
FinishMatte (not glossy)
BrightnessSlightly warm (off-white, not pure white)
Opacity100% (no show-through)

Binding Specification

Option A: Screw Post (Recommended)

  • 2 posts, 0.25" diameter, hidden brass or stainless steel
  • Posts positioned 0.5" from left edge, 0.75" from top & bottom
  • Allows page changes (upgrade-friendly)
  • Premium feel

Option B: Spiral Coil

  • Lies completely flat (customer convenience)
  • More casual appearance
  • Good for frequent updates

Finishing Options

  • Foil Stamping (Recommended): Gold or silver foil on cover logo/title (+$0.75–1.50 per unit)
  • Debossing: Logo pressed into cover (+$0.50–1.00 per unit)
  • Embossing: Logo raised on cover (+$0.50–1.00 per unit)
  • Texture: Subtle embossed texture on cover (+$0.25–0.50 per unit)
Note: Synthetic paper is already waterproof and tear-resistant — no lamination needed. Laminating would add cost and thickness without benefit.

Prototype Execution Plan

Phase 1: Design Finalization (2–3 Days)

  • Step 1: Confirm all menu items, pricing, and signatures with owner
  • Step 2: Gather/shoot 5 hero photos (Shaking Beef, Garlic Noodles, Vermicelli, Coffee, Egg Rolls)
  • Step 3: Create design files in InDesign or Canva (all 16 pages)
  • Step 4: Export to high-quality PDF (CMYK, 300 DPI, bleeds included)

Phase 2: Print Prototype (1–2 Days)

  • Step 5: Get quotes from 2–3 local printers (confirm synthetic paper capability)
  • Step 6: Order 3–4 sample menus with foil stamping and binding
  • Step 7: Inspect quality (color, binding, foil) when received

Phase 3: Owner Feedback (1–2 Days)

  • Step 8: Present all prototypes to owner
  • Step 9: Get specific feedback:
    • Does this feel premium?
    • Is the hierarchy clear?
    • Are descriptions helpful?
    • Should any dishes move/change?
  • Step 10: Iterate if needed (reprint 1 revised version for testing)

Phase 4: Production (5–7 Days)

  • Step 11: Finalize all changes and approve designs
  • Step 12: Place order for full run (50–100 menus)
  • Step 13: Quality assurance on delivery
  • Step 14: Deliver to restaurant
Total Timeline: 10–14 days from design start to full production delivery.
