Welcome to

Grandpa Táo Kitchen

— Asian fusion, El Cajon —

One kitchen. Many traditions. We cook what we love to eat.

Our menu draws from Vietnam first — slow-simmered phở, fresh bún bowls, lemongrass marinades, and bánh mì pressed to order — and from the wider Asian table our cooks grew up with: Cantonese fried rice, Japanese sushi, Thai-style iced tea, and the kind of crispy chicken you crave on a Tuesday night.

We use top-quality ingredients, cook everything to order, and don't shortcut the broth — twelve hours, every morning. Whether you're here for a quick bánh mì at lunch or a long phở at the end of a long day, we're glad you came.

If you don't see what you want, ask. We'll make it the way you like.

— from the kitchen of Grandpa Táo

Shaking Beef
Bò Lúc Lắc
R24 · $18.25

Filet mignon shaken in a roaring hot skillet with garlic, butter, and freshly cracked black pepper. Served sizzling on a cast-iron plate over a bed of watercress, with steamed jasmine rice on the side.

The name comes from the motion — we shake the pan, hard, while the meat sears. The result is a crust on the outside and a blush of pink inside, the kind of dish that gets people leaning over from the next table to ask what you ordered.
